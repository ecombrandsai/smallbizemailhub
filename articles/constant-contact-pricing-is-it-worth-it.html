Constant Contact Pricing Plans Broken Down

Constant Contact currently offers three paid plans: Lite, Standard, and Premium. Every plan is priced based on the number of contacts in your list. Here is what you get at the entry-level contact count for each tier.

Lite Plan — Starting at $12/month (up to 500 contacts)

The Lite plan is designed for businesses that are just getting started with email marketing. You get unlimited email sends, access to over 200 customizable templates, a drag-and-drop editor, basic list management, and tracking for open rates and click rates. You also get one user login. There are no automations on this tier, but for a business sending a weekly newsletter or a monthly promotion, that is rarely a dealbreaker at first.

Standard Plan — Starting at $35/month (up to 500 contacts)

The Standard plan adds email automations, which is the feature that separates occasional senders from businesses that actually use email to drive repeat revenue. You can set up welcome sequences for new subscribers, birthday emails, anniversary offers, and re-engagement flows for contacts who have not opened anything in 90 days. You also get A/B subject line testing and list segmentation, which lets you send a "bring your dog" promotion only to customers who have mentioned pets. Three user logins are included.

Premium Plan — Starting at $80/month (up to 500 contacts)

Premium is built for businesses with bigger lists and more sophisticated needs. You get everything in Standard plus custom automation paths, advanced segmentation, Google Ads integration, and up to 10 user logins. If you manage email marketing for multiple locations or have a marketing team of more than three people, Premium starts to earn its price. For a solo operator with a list under 2,000, it is probably more than you need.

Who Should Actually Pay for Constant Contact

Constant Contact is the right choice for you if you fit any of these descriptions:

You run a local service business — salon, HVAC company, restaurant, gym, dental practice. These businesses rely on repeat customers, and email is the cheapest way to stay top of mind between appointments. Constant Contact's event management feature is particularly useful for businesses that host classes, workshops, or seasonal promotions. You can sell event tickets and manage RSVPs directly inside the platform.

You are not technical and do not want to spend two hours reading documentation before you can send your first email. Constant Contact's onboarding flow is the most guided in the industry. It walks you through importing your contacts, picking a template, and sending a test email in under 30 minutes. We have seen complete beginners go from sign-up to first campaign in one sitting.

You value phone support. This is a bigger deal than it sounds. If your campaign is going out on Black Friday and your image block is not rendering, you need to reach a person, not wait 24 hours for a ticket response. Constant Contact's support team is US-based and available by phone Monday through Friday.

You have a nonprofit or sell e-commerce products on a platform like Shopify or WooCommerce. Constant Contact integrates natively with both. Nonprofits also get a 20 percent discount on all plans, bringing the Lite plan down to under $10 per month.

Constant Contact is probably not the best fit if you are a solo blogger with a list under 500 and you only send one email per month. In that case, Mailchimp's free plan may serve you fine until your list grows. But if you are running any kind of real business with real customers, the $12 entry price is not a burden — it is a business expense with a measurable return.

How to Get the Most Value Out of Constant Contact's Price

Signing up is the easy part. Getting a return on the subscription requires a few practical habits. Here is what the small businesses we talk to do differently when they see real results from email marketing.

1. Send on a schedule, not just when you remember

The businesses that get the worst ROI from email marketing are the ones that send three emails in January and then go silent until March. Your subscribers forget who you are. Open rates drop. Unsubscribes tick up. Pick a cadence — weekly, biweekly, or monthly — and stick to it. Constant Contact's content calendar view makes it easy to plan a month of emails in one session.

2. Use the welcome email automation even on the Lite plan

Actually, scratch that — upgrade to Standard for the automations. The welcome email is the single highest-performing email you will ever send. Open rates on welcome emails average 50 to 60 percent, compared to 20 percent for regular campaigns. A welcome sequence that introduces your business, shares your best offer, and asks a question gets new subscribers engaged before they have a chance to forget why they signed up.

3. Clean your list every 90 days

Paying for contacts who never open your emails is money out the window and, more importantly, a signal to inbox providers that your emails are unwanted. Inside Constant Contact, filter for contacts who have not opened anything in 90 days. Send them a re-engagement email with a subject line like "Still want to hear from us?" Anyone who does not open or click that email should be removed from your active list. You will pay less, and your open rates will climb, which improves deliverability for everyone who stays.

4. A/B test your subject lines (Standard plan)

A subject line change from "Our spring sale is here" to "48 hours only: 20% off your next visit" can double your open rate. That is not an exaggeration — we have seen clients go from 18 percent open rates to 34 percent with a single subject line change. Constant Contact's A/B testing sends each version to a subset of your list and automatically delivers the winner to everyone else. You set it up in about three minutes.

5. Connect your e-commerce store on day one

If you sell anything online — Shopify, WooCommerce, Etsy, Squarespace — connect it to Constant Contact immediately. Once it is connected, Constant Contact can automatically send abandoned cart reminders, post-purchase thank-you emails, and product recommendation emails without you doing anything. One furniture store we profiled added $800 in monthly revenue just from the abandoned cart reminder sequence they set up in 20 minutes.

Frequently Asked Questions About Constant Contact Pricing

How much does Constant Contact actually cost per month?

Constant Contact's Lite plan starts at $12 per month for up to 500 contacts. The Standard plan starts at $35 per month, and the Premium plan starts at $80 per month. Prices scale upward as your contact list grows. You can try any plan free for 60 days before committing, which is one of the longest free trials in the industry and gives you real time to test results before paying anything.

Does Constant Contact offer a free plan?

Constant Contact does not have a permanent free plan, but it offers a 60-day free trial with no credit card required. During the trial you can send up to 100 emails and test most core features. After 60 days you choose a paid plan or your account pauses. This trial period is longer than Mailchimp's 30-day trial window and gives you time to run a real campaign and measure results before committing.

Is Constant Contact worth it for a business with fewer than 500 contacts?

Yes. At $12 per month for the Lite plan, the cost is easy to justify even at low list sizes. If one email campaign brings back a single customer who spends $20, you have already covered your monthly cost with $8 to spare. Most small businesses with even a modest open rate of 20 percent see a return far higher than the monthly subscription fee. Small lists often convert at higher rates because the relationship is more personal.

What happens to my price as my contact list grows?

Constant Contact uses tiered contact-based pricing. On the Lite plan, 500 contacts costs $12 per month, 1,000 contacts costs $30, 2,500 contacts costs $50, and 5,000 contacts costs $80. You can check exact current pricing on Constant Contact's pricing page. Upgrading tiers is straightforward inside your account dashboard with no contract lock-in required unless you chose an annual prepay option for the discount.

Can I cancel Constant Contact at any time?

Yes. Constant Contact is month-to-month with no long-term contract unless you opt into an annual plan for a discount. You can cancel any time from your account settings or by calling support. There are no cancellation fees. If you prepay annually and cancel early, Constant Contact prorates a refund for unused months, which is a fairer policy than some competitors who keep the full prepayment regardless of when you cancel.
