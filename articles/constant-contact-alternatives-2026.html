  • Automation quality — Not just "does it have automation" but how many clicks does it take to set up a simple welcome series.

Mailchimp — Strong Templates, Weaker Support

Mailchimp is the most recognised name in email marketing and for good reason — its template library is excellent and the drag-and-drop editor is intuitive. The problem is that once you need help, you are largely on your own. Phone support is not available on most plans, and its customer service email response times averaged 18 hours in our testing. Mailchimp also removed its free automation features in 2024, so you now need a paid plan to run even a simple welcome email. At $13 per month for 500 contacts it is marginally pricier than Constant Contact's entry plan, and you get less for it.

Brevo — Great Value, Rougher Experience

Brevo (formerly Sendinblue) offers one of the best free tiers in the industry — up to 300 emails per day with no contact limit. That sounds generous until you realise 300 emails per day is only about 9,000 emails per month, which means a list of 2,000 contacts can only be emailed four times a month at most. The paid Starter plan at $9 per month removes the daily cap and is genuinely good value. The interface is functional but feels dated compared to Constant Contact, and automation setup requires more technical comfort than most small business owners have.

Klaviyo — The E-Commerce Powerhouse

If you run a Shopify or WooCommerce store with more than 1,000 products and want to trigger emails based on what specific items a customer viewed, Klaviyo is unmatched. Its product-level segmentation and abandoned cart flows are genuinely impressive. But the setup time is substantial — plan on a full weekend to get your flows working properly — and pricing climbs fast. At 5,000 contacts you are paying $100 per month. For a non-technical business owner selling one or two product lines, Klaviyo is overkill and expensive overkill at that.

AWeber — Reliable but Dated

AWeber has been around since 1998 and that heritage shows — the platform is rock solid and the phone support is genuinely helpful. However, the template designs look noticeably older than competitors, and its automation builder has not kept pace with the industry. At $12.50 per month for 500 contacts it sits right next to Constant Contact in price but delivers less on the automation and design fronts. It is a fine choice if reliability and phone support are your only criteria, but Constant Contact beats it on nearly every other metric.

How to Switch Email Platforms Without Losing Your List or Your Mind

Whether you are moving to Constant Contact from another tool, or you are leaving Constant Contact and want to know how this process works, here is the exact four-step process that takes most people less than an hour.

Step 1: Export your current list as a CSV. Every major email platform has an export function. In Mailchimp it is under Audience → Manage Contacts → Export. In Klaviyo it is under Lists → Export List. Download the file and save it somewhere you will find it.

Step 2: Clean the list before you import. Dead email addresses hurt your deliverability score on your new platform right from day one. Run your CSV through a free tool like NeverBounce's free checker (1,000 verifications free) or ZeroBounce. Remove any hard bounces and addresses flagged as risky.

Step 3: Import into Constant Contact. From your dashboard, click Contacts → Import. You can drag and drop the CSV file. Constant Contact will map the column headers automatically in most cases. The import for a 2,000-contact list takes about three minutes.

Step 4: Set up a re-engagement email before your first campaign. Any time you move lists between platforms, send a short "we have moved" email on day one that reminds subscribers who you are, confirms they still want to hear from you, and gives them a one-click option to stay subscribed. This protects your sender reputation on the new platform and clears out any contacts who have gone cold.

Constant Contact also offers a free data migration call if you have a large or complex list. You can book it directly after you sign up.
