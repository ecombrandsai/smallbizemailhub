MailerLite Review 2026 - Honest Assessment for Small Business Owners

MailerLite is the underdog favorite of the email marketing industry — cleaner UI than Mailchimp, friendlier price than Constant Contact, and a genuinely useful free tier. After 90 days of side-by-side testing, we landed on 4.0/5 stars. Real strengths, real limits. The verdict: MailerLite is excellent for solopreneurs, bloggers, and side hustles. For a real small business with employees and customers, we recommend Constant Contact.

MailerLite earns its 4.0 with one of the cleanest UIs in the industry, a genuinely usable free tier, and pricing that rewards smaller senders. Where it loses points: no phone support at any tier, smaller integration ecosystem than the leaders, and slightly lower deliverability than our top pick. What MailerLite Does Well

The cleanest UI in the industry

If user experience design is your hobby, MailerLite is genuinely a pleasure to use. Every interaction feels considered. Animations are subtle. Buttons land where you expect them. Setup time was the fastest in our test — 22 minutes from signup to first send, beating Constant Contact's 28 minutes by a small margin.

Genuine free plan up to 1,000 contacts (with real features)

Most "free" tiers in the email marketing world are loss leaders designed to push you to paid. MailerLite's free tier is more generous than most. 1,000 contacts and 12,000 monthly sends covers most solopreneurs and side hustles for the first 6-12 months. The catch: no automations, support is limited, and MailerLite branding shows in the footer.

Includes websites and landing pages — useful for solopreneurs

MailerLite includes a website builder (yes, really) and landing page builder at every paid tier. For a solopreneur who doesn't have a separate website host, this single feature can replace a $15/mo Squarespace subscription. Constant Contact has landing pages but no full website builder.

Polished drag-and-drop builder

MailerLite's drag-and-drop builder rivals Mailchimp's for visual polish. Templates skew modern, minimalist, and content-brand-friendly. For a blogger, podcaster, or course creator, the design experience is excellent.

Affordable for low-volume senders

$10/mo entry price (vs $12 Constant Contact, $13 Mailchimp Essentials). At 500 contacts, MailerLite is the cheapest paid tier among the major players. The savings are real, if small.

Where MailerLite Falls Short

Phone support doesn't exist — chat only, paid tier only

This is the biggest gap for small businesses. MailerLite offers chat support on paid tiers and email support on the free tier. No phone support at any price. For a small business owner who needs a human on the line when something breaks at 11pm on a Sunday, this is a real limitation.

Smaller integration ecosystem than older players

MailerLite's integration list is solid but smaller than Mailchimp's or Constant Contact's. Shopify and WooCommerce are covered. Square's there. But less common POS systems, niche CRMs, and specialized industry tools may not have native integrations — and you'll need Zapier to bridge the gap.

Free tier has limits that creep up on you

The free tier looks generous on paper. In practice: no automations means no welcome series, no abandoned cart, no birthday email. Most growing small businesses outgrow the free tier within 60-90 days. By the time you need automations (which is most small businesses by month two), you're paying anyway — and paying for chat-only support.

Less battle-tested for high-stakes business sending

MailerLite is excellent for solopreneurs, bloggers, and side hustles. For a business where one mis-sent campaign can damage real revenue, the lack of phone support and lower-but-still-good deliverability (95.3% vs Constant Contact's 97.4% in our test) matter more.

How It Compares to Constant Contact

MailerLite and Constant Contact target slightly different customers — and the right pick depends on which one you are.

  • MailerLite wins on: UI polish, free tier generosity, entry price ($10 vs $12), website builder.
  • Constant Contact wins on: Phone support, deliverability (97.4% vs 95.3%), list import forgiveness, events tool, 60-day trial, industry-organized templates, small-business focus.

The decisive factor for most small businesses is the support story. MailerLite's "no phone support at any tier" is fine for solopreneurs. For a real business with employees, customers, and revenue depending on email working — it's a meaningful gap.

Frequently Asked Questions

Is MailerLite worth it in 2026?

Yes, for solopreneurs and bloggers. The clean UI, real free tier, and competitive pricing make it one of the strongest tools for very small operations. For businesses with employees and customers, Constant Contact's phone support and feature set tip the balance.

Is MailerLite really free?

Yes, the free plan covers up to 1,000 contacts and 12,000 monthly sends. The catch: no automations, no support, no template variety beyond the basics, and MailerLite branding in the footer.

Is MailerLite better than Constant Contact?

MailerLite has a cleaner UI and a lower entry price. Constant Contact wins on phone support, deliverability, list import forgiveness, events tool, and small-business-focused features. For real small businesses, Constant Contact is the better fit.

Does MailerLite have phone support?

No. MailerLite offers chat support on paid tiers and email support on the free tier. Phone support is not available at any tier.

What's the best alternative to MailerLite for a small business?

Constant Contact is our top alternative for small businesses. The 60-day free trial, included phone support, and small-business-focused features make it the better pick for any business with employees and customers.
