Constant Contact vs HubSpot: The Head-to-Head Breakdown

Let's get into the specifics. Here is how each platform performs across the categories that matter most to small business owners.

Pricing

Constant Contact starts at $12 per month for up to 500 contacts. That price includes unlimited emails, basic automations, a drag-and-drop editor, and phone and chat support. The price scales with your contact count, not with arbitrary feature gates.

HubSpot has a free tier, but it is heavily restricted: 2,000 email sends per month, HubSpot branding on every email, and almost no automation. When you move to a paid plan, the Starter tier begins at $20 per month but only includes 1,000 marketing contacts and very limited automation. To access the automation features that actually save you time, you need the Professional plan at $890 per month. That is not a typo. For most small businesses, that gap is the end of the conversation.

Ease of Use

Constant Contact is genuinely simple. The dashboard is clean, the template library has over 200 mobile-responsive designs sorted by industry and goal, and the drag-and-drop editor works the way you expect it to. New users consistently report sending their first campaign on the same day they sign up.

HubSpot's interface reflects its complexity. Email lives inside the Marketing Hub, which lives inside the broader HubSpot CRM. Finding the right screen requires navigating menus designed for marketing professionals who use the platform every day. If you are not using the CRM features, you are paying for real estate you never visit.

Automation

Constant Contact includes welcome email sequences, birthday and anniversary messages, resend-to-non-openers, and e-commerce triggered emails on its entry-level paid plans. These are the automations that make a real difference for small businesses: someone joins your list, they get a welcome offer automatically. You do not have to be there.

HubSpot's automation engine is more powerful, but it only becomes accessible on plans that cost hundreds of dollars per month. If you are comparing the two platforms at similar price points, Constant Contact's automation is more practical and more usable.

Deliverability

Constant Contact maintains inbox delivery rates above 97 percent in independent testing. The company has a dedicated deliverability team, proactively monitors sender reputation across its platform, and maintains strong relationships with major ISPs. For small businesses who cannot afford a technical team monitoring bounce rates, this matters.

HubSpot delivers well too, but results vary more across plan tiers and are more sensitive to list hygiene issues that small business owners may not catch early.

Customer Support

Constant Contact offers phone support, live chat, and email on every paid plan. Their support team is known for being patient with non-technical users, which is exactly what you need when something goes wrong the morning of a big campaign.

HubSpot's support is tiered. Free and Starter users get community forum access and chatbot help. Phone support is reserved for Professional and Enterprise customers. If you pay $20 per month and have an urgent problem on a Tuesday afternoon, you are on your own.

When HubSpot Actually Makes Sense

To be fair to HubSpot, there is a specific type of business where it makes more sense than Constant Contact. If you have a sales team that needs to track deals, calls, and follow-ups inside the same system as your marketing campaigns, HubSpot's CRM integration is genuinely powerful. The ability to see that a contact opened your last three emails, visited your pricing page, and is currently in a sales pipeline stage is useful information for a sales rep.

If you have a marketing budget over $1,000 per month to spend on software, a dedicated marketing manager, and a need to run multi-channel campaigns across email, ads, social, and landing pages from a single platform, HubSpot's Professional tier starts to make economic sense.

But if you are a small business owner making decisions about a $12-per-month vs. $890-per-month tool, the choice is clear. You do not need what HubSpot is selling at that price. And the free tier, while tempting, will hit its limits within a month of serious use.

How to Get the Most Out of Constant Contact From Day One

Getting set up with Constant Contact is fast, but there are a few steps that make a real difference to your results from the first campaign.

Step 1: Import your contact list properly

Constant Contact lets you import contacts from a CSV file, from Gmail, Outlook, Salesforce, or directly from your Shopify or WooCommerce store. Do not skip the list segmentation step during import. Even a basic split between customers who have bought from you and people who just signed up for your newsletter will allow you to send more relevant messages from day one.

Step 2: Set up your welcome email before you do anything else

The welcome email is the single highest-performing email in any sequence. Open rates average over 50 percent, compared to 20 to 25 percent for regular campaigns. In Constant Contact, you can build a welcome automation in under 10 minutes. Go to Campaigns, click Create, select Automated Email Series, and choose Welcome Email. Write one email that tells new subscribers what to expect, offers them something of value, and invites a reply. That is all you need to start.

Step 3: Send on a consistent schedule

Consistency beats frequency. An email once a week that people expect and look forward to outperforms three emails a week that feel random. Decide on your send day and time, stick to it for 60 days, and you will see your open rates stabilise and grow as subscribers learn to expect your emails.

Step 4: Use the resend-to-non-openers feature

Constant Contact has a built-in feature that resends your campaign with a different subject line to subscribers who did not open the first version. This alone typically increases your total campaign reach by 15 to 25 percent with zero extra work on your end. You will find it in the campaign results screen after your first send.

Step 5: Check your reports after every campaign

Constant Contact's reporting shows you open rate, click rate, bounces, unsubscribes, and which links got clicked. Check these numbers after every campaign. The subject lines with higher open rates and the link types with higher click rates tell you exactly what your audience wants more of. Let your subscribers teach you what to send next.

Frequently Asked Questions: Constant Contact vs HubSpot

Is HubSpot really free for email marketing?

HubSpot has a free tier, but it limits you to 2,000 emails per month and puts HubSpot branding on every message. Once you need more sends, remove branding, or unlock automations, prices jump sharply. For most small businesses sending regular campaigns, Constant Contact at $12 per month delivers far more practical value without those restrictions.

Which tool is easier to set up for a beginner?

Constant Contact wins on ease of setup. You can import contacts, pick a template, and send your first email in under an hour. HubSpot's interface is built around a full CRM, so you have to learn where email lives inside a larger system. That is fine if you need CRM features, but it adds unnecessary steps for pure email marketing.

Does Constant Contact integrate with my existing tools?

Yes. Constant Contact connects with over 300 apps including Shopify, WooCommerce, QuickBooks, Salesforce, Canva, and WordPress. Most integrations are one-click and take minutes. If you run an e-commerce store, the Shopify sync automatically adds new customers to your list and lets you send abandoned-cart emails without any coding.

What happens to my data if I switch tools later?

Both Constant Contact and HubSpot let you export your contact list as a CSV at any time. Your emails and campaign reports stay inside the platform you leave, so screenshot or download important stats before you cancel. Switching is not technically complicated, but plan a quiet period like after a big campaign so you do not interrupt active sequences.

Which platform gives better deliverability rates?

Constant Contact consistently ranks among the top platforms for inbox deliverability, with independent tests showing rates above 97 percent. HubSpot performs well too, but deliverability depends heavily on your plan tier and list hygiene. Constant Contact's dedicated deliverability team proactively monitors sender reputation, which matters most for small businesses without a technical team.
